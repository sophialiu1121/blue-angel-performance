The Founding Story
"I reached my ceiling as an "elite runner". Now I want to build the foundation and raise others."

Blue Angel Performance was built on a simple belief: elite-level training science should not be reserved for elite athletes. After qualifying for the Olympic Trials Marathon and completing a Ph.D. in Exercise Physiology at UW, the move to coaching was inevitable.

The coaching philosophy was proven by results. One athlete improved their marathon by over 40 minutes — from 4 hours to sub-3. That athlete had no serious running background — which turned out to be the biggest advantage. No old habits. No ego about pace. Just clear instruction and willingness to trust the process.

Easier to adapt. Quicker to break old habits. Faster to improve. Inspired by elite crew culture — CNW, Shenjing Bing — this summer we build speed. Then we build marathoners.

The Science

Training
Science

Understanding these five systems is the difference between training hard and training smart. Every session targets one or more deliberately.

01

LT1 — Aerobic Threshold

~2 mmol/L lactate. The conversational pace you could sustain for hours. Raising LT1 raises your aerobic engine permanently. Elite marathoners race only 10–15% above their LT1. Trained by Zone 2 easy runs and long runs.

02

LT2 — Lactate Threshold

~4 mmol/L. Fastest sustainable pace for 30–60 minutes. Directly determines 10K to marathon race pace. Trained by tempo runs and cruise intervals — the cornerstone of competitive distance running.

03

VO₂max

Your aerobic ceiling. vVO₂max ≈ 5K race pace. Interval sessions push the ceiling higher. +8–12% gain in 8 weeks. Trained by Billat 30/30s and 4-minute reps. Limit to once per week.

04

Fatigue Resistance

The ability to hold pace when everything hurts. Trained by long runs with pace finishes, back-to-back sessions, and pre-fatigued intervals. Separates runners who race from those who survive.

05

Fat vs. Carbohydrate Metabolism

Glycogen lasts ~90–120 min. Fat stores fuel 20+ hours. The wall is glycogen depletion — fat metabolism delays it. Trained by Zone 2 volume and fasted runs. At race intensity, carbohydrate is the primary fuel. Elite runners are metabolically flexible: burning fat efficiently at 70–75% VO₂max and switching to carbs at race pace. The 80/20 rule: 80% of training at LT1 or below. This is what makes the 20% hard work effective.
